Leg Exercises

Your guide to building those legs!

Barbell Squats

  1. Stand with your feet more than shoulder-width apart and hold a barbell across your upper back with an overhand grip - avoid resting it on your neck. Hug the bar into your traps to engage your upper back muscles
  2. Slowly sit back into a squat with head up, back straight and backside out. Lower until your hips are aligned with your knees, with your legs at 90 degrees - a deeper squat will beneficial but focus on form first.
  3. Drive your heels into the floor to push yourself explosively back up. Keep form until youre stood up straight: 1 rep.

Leg Press

The leg press is an easy way for you to work your whole leg!

  1. Sit down on a leg press machine and place your legs omn the platform in fornt of you. Your feet should ne postioned approximately a foot to one and a half feet apart.
  2. Lower the safety bars holding the platform in place. Press the platform all the way up until your legs are fully extemded, wihtoput locking your knees. This will be your starting postion
  3. As you inhale, slowley l;ower the platform until your upper and lower legs make a 90-degree angle.
  4. Push with the heels of your feet and use your quadriceps to go back to the starting position. Exhale as much as you do.
  5. Repeat for the number of reps in your set. Make sure that the safety pins are locked properly oonce you have finished.

Romanian Deadlifts

Romanian Deadlits or RDLs are a great way to build up that hamstring muscle!

  1. Stand behind a grounded barbell. Bend your knees slightly to grab it, keep your shins, back and hips straight
  2. Without bending your back, push your hips forwards to lift the bar
  3. From upright, push your hips back to lower the bar, bending your knees only slightly
