Back Exercises

Your guide to effective arm workouts

Bent Over Rows

  1. Grab a barbell with an overhand grip, hands slightly wider than shoulder width apart.
  2. RWith your legs slightly bent, keep your back perfectly straight and bend your upper body forward until it’s almost perpendicular to the floor.
  3. From here row the weight upwards into the lower part of your chest. Pause. And return under control to the start position.

Pull Up

If you want a V-shape physique — you do, that’s why you’re here — then there’s no avoiding pull-ups.

  1. Grab the handles of the pull-up station with your palms facing away from you and your arms fully extended.
  2. Your hands should be around shoulder-width apart.
  3. Squeeze your shoulder blades together, exhale and drive your elbows towards your hips to bring your chin above the bar.
  4. Lower under control back to the start position.

Lat Pulldowns

Just like pull-ups, lat pull-downs — a firm bodybuilding favourite — will build your lats, while working at a slow tempo will maximise your muscle gain. Keep form strict and reap the rewards. A tip: always bring the bar in front of your head. The behind-the-neck version can damage your rotator cuff.

  1. Kneel or sit on a bench in front of the cable machine
  2. KGrab the bar with your palms facing away from you, shoulder-width apart
  3. Lean back slightly and push your chest out.
  4. Pull the bar down to your chest, then return slowly to the start position. Your torso should remain still throughout.
